Help - Welcome to your CRA account

We’ve made it easier to access and manage your CRA accounts. This page explains what you see on the Welcome page when you sign in and how to use its features.

What's on the new Welcome Page

When you sign in, the Welcome page displays panels for each account linked to your Social Insurance Number (SIN). These may include:

  • My Account: For your personal tax and benefit information
  • My Business Account: Your default account for managing your business tax details
  • Represent a Client Account: If you represent other individuals or businesses

To access an account:

  1. Click the panel for the account you want.
  2. You’ll go directly to the account’s overview page.

To switch between your business accounts, use the menu in the top left corner of your default business account overview.

Features of the Welcome page

  • What’s New – Key updates or changes you need to know
  • Add Account – Register a business, add a business number to your profile, register yourself or business as a representative or add a repID to your profile
  • Mail icons – Click the mail icons in each panel to go directly to your account’s mail. An indicator is showing up if you have unread mail in your personal or business accounts
  • Security Settings - Manage your CRA security options, personal identification number (PIN) and multi-factor authentication settings

Managing Your Security Settings

You can adjust all your security settings directly from the Welcome page. These settings now apply to all your CRA accounts.

CRA security options

This option is only be available if you signed in with a CRA user ID and password. After selecting Continue, you will go to a page that gives instructions on how to “Manage your CRA security options”.

To manage your CRA security options:

  1. Exit the portal
  2. Sign in again with your CRA user ID and password
  3. After answering the security question, select the button Manage CRA security options from the “CRA sign in and security options” page.
, you must exit the portal and then sign in again.

Available Options:

  • Change CRA user ID
  • Change CRA password
  • Change CRA security questions and answers
  • Update additional security feature preference
  • Revoke CRA user ID
  • View the Terms and conditions of use
  • View recent CRA login history

Personal identification number (PIN)

You can edit, delete, or create a PIN for My Account:

  • Select Create to add a PIN to your account. Your PIN must be 6 numbers without spaces and cannot be the first digits of your social insurance number.
  • If you already have an existing PIN, you can select Edit to change it. You will need to use your new PIN next time you call the CRA.
  • Select Delete to remove your PIN.

>Using a PIN provides added security and will reduce your time on the phone because we’ll be able to confirm your identity before you speak to a CRA agent or access the automated line.

Multi-factor authentication settings

To modify your settings, select Continue to go to the "Manage multi-factor authentication – passcode options” page.

Select the passcode option you want to modify:

  • Telephone: To add, modify, or remove a telephone number
    • Add up to 3 different numbers
    • - Choose your preferred the language to receive your one-time passcode
  • Passcode grid: To view, add or delete a passcode grid
    • If you don’t have a passcode grid, you can Generate passcode grid. You need to save it as a PDF or print it, and keep it for 18 months
    • If you already have one, you can choose Delete grid or Delete and generate new grid

Enroll in multiple multi-factor authentication (MFA) options

We recommend that you enroll in more than one option and that you add more than one phone number.

When you sign in, you can choose between the following option to get your one-time passcode:

  • Get a text on your cell
  • Get a call on your landline
  • Provide information from your passcode grid
  • Use the authenticator app

Enrolling in multiple MFA options will help ensure that you can still access the CRA sign-in services if you change your phone number, misplace your passcode grid, or delete the third-party authenticator app.

For more details, go to Multi-factor authentication (MFA) – CRA sign-in services help.
